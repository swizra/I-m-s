Accessibility Statement

KEFE aims to provide a storefront experience that is readable, navigable, and usable across different devices, assistive technologies, and input methods.

Focus areas

Readable content, clear navigation, responsive layouts, and keyboard-friendly controls.

Report issues

accessibility@kefe.ch

Accessibility approach

We aim to align new interface work with widely used accessibility principles, including meaningful structure, visible focus states, understandable controls, and layouts that remain usable on smaller screens and zoomed displays.

What we try to support

  • Logical page structure and clear headings.
  • Readable contrast and mobile-friendly layouts.
  • Navigation that works without relying on one single input style.
  • Forms, buttons, and dialogs that can be understood without visual guesswork.

Testing and continuous improvement

Accessibility is reviewed as part of ongoing frontend work. We use manual checks on navigation, focus behavior, and responsive layouts, and we refine pages when issues are found through feedback or internal review.

Known limits

Some parts of the storefront may still be improved over time, especially where live product content, media, or advanced interactions are involved. Feedback helps prioritize the most meaningful fixes.

How to contact us

If a page, control, or workflow is difficult to use, please share the page name, device type, browser, and a short description of the issue so we can reproduce it.

Response commitment

Accessibility reports are reviewed by the frontend and support team together. We aim to acknowledge new reports within 2 business days and prioritize fixes based on severity and customer impact.
